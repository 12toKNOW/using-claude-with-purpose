Why this guide exists.

Most material written about using Claude — or any large language model — falls into one of two buckets.

The first is academic. It examines AI adoption at the organizational level and produces frameworks for workforce strategy. It is valuable for scholars. It does not help the executive sitting at the keyboard on Monday morning trying to get useful output.

The second is technical. It explains how to install the tool, configure Projects, and write good prompts if you are a developer. It assumes a technical fluency most senior leaders do not have and do not need to acquire.

This guide occupies the space in between. It is written for the executive who is not a technologist and not a scholar — someone running a business, leading a team, making decisions with real consequences. It is about judgment: how to ask, how to iterate, when to trust the output, when to walk away.

The question is not whether AI can help you. It is whether you will use it in ways that compound value — or in ways that slowly erode the judgment you were hired for.

What is inside.

  • The five categories of serious executive use, and how each succeeds differently.
  • The Context Ladder — why most users prompt at Rung 2 when they should be at Rung 4.
  • The four question types every conversation falls into, and how to prompt each.
  • The three checks before acting on any output — source, SME, reversibility.
  • The five situations when not to use the tool at all.
  • The distinction between augmentation and outsourcing — and how to tell which side you are on.

For whom this is written.

Senior leaders who are using AI seriously and have noticed that the existing guidance does not quite fit. Executives building real artifacts and making real decisions with the tool — not playing with features. Anyone trying to use AI in a way that compounds judgment rather than erodes it.

Every pattern, strength, and weakness described in the guide is drawn from real executive use. No hypotheticals, no feature tours, no vendor positioning.
